Why not Google Analytics Tags?

   Google Analytics — is a tool for Marketing and SEO teams. It is about to help teams with conversions. It helps to get answers on questions like:

  • Do we have visitors on our website?
  • If yes, which pages do they visit?
  • How many of them?
  • From which page to which page do they go?
  • Do they "convert" (signup, buy, download, leave email, etc.)?
  • Where do they come from (search, social network, country, city)?
  • What browser they use?
  • What is their screen size?
  • etc.

   Such kind of questions. For abstract "they".

   Do They Use — is for Product teams. It is about to help teams to do what matters from UX point of view. What to develop and what to get rid of.

   For example, take a look at this screen of "Brawl Stars" smartphone game account. It has more than 20 active controls:

   Do They Use helps to answer on questions like:

  • Do they use all these elements?
  • Which elements are used the most often? Which ones are used the least?
  • Which elements are used by most users? Which ones are used by the least?
  • What options they choose most often? Which ones they never choose?
  • New feature that we've made — does it get traction?
  • What elements and features need our attention?
  • What started to have anomaly of usage: too high / too low usage?
  • Who of them actually use this? Do they buy or they just free trial users?
  • etc.

   Such kind of questions. For exact you know who are "they" and why you should care.

Vision for this service. Where it goes

   Hierarchy. As for now service monitors only elements usage. Stats are flat (not hierarchical) and have no visual comparison to each other (to see which elements are used the most and which the least). That's why next steps are to display hierarchical models of data (button "Cancel" is on that page in that place, it has path) and show elements usage volumes compared to each other (show sankey diagrams).

   Data. Interacting with elements users choose options and enter data. We want to display analysis for the data as well. For example, to answer on questions like: "Which options are most often selected in dropdown list and which never selected?".

   Features usage. Elements here and there — are the parts of more global things — features. Features could be tagged and monitored and analysed as well. Same as elements features are hierarchical items.

   Users. Okey, someone uses that element or feature or enters that data into the input field. Why should we care at all? Maybe it's just QA or PM or UX designer or Sales or Marketing team interacts with your app or service in prod. We want stats to include UID(s) and UGID(s) — so you could filter only users and users groups that matter or exclude users and groups that irrelevant. Don't optimise something just because "someone uses this". Know who uses "this" and optimise for those who matters.
