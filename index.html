Difference with Google Analytics

Google Analytics — is for Marketing

Google Analytics — is a tool for Marketing and Product Marketing analytics. It helps teams to make marketing decisions. Google Analytics helps to get answers on the questions like:

  • Do we have visitors on our website and/or mobile app?
  • If yes, which pages/screens do they visit? And how many times?
  • How many of them?
  • How much time do they spend on each page?
  • Did they view the page, did they scroll?
  • Which page to page paths do they usually navigate?
  • Do they "convert" (signup, buy, download, leave a email, etc.)? And how many?
  • Where do they come from (organic search, advertisements, social networks, etc.)?
  • Where do they come from (country, city)?
  • What platforms do they use (desktop, tablet, smartphone)?
  • What browsers do they use?
  • What is their screen resolution size?
  • What is about retention, engagement and lifetime value?
  • etc.

Such kind of Marketing analytics questions.

Do They Use — is for Product

Do They Use — is an app for Product Management and Product Development. It helps teams make product decisions to do what matters from Product and UX points of view. Do They Use helps teams to get answers on questions "what to develop next and why" based on their app real usage data.

For example, take a look at this screen of "Brawl Stars" smartphone game made by "Supercell". It has more than 20 active controls:

Do They Use app helps to get answers on the questions like:

  • Do they use all these elements?
  • Which elements do they use most often?
  • Which elements they never use?
  • Does our new feature or web element gets traction? How it goes live?
  • What elements need our attention (because of high or low usage)?
  • What elements started to have anomaly of usage: too high or too low usage? Maybe we released some bug and now we have such anomaly there?
  • etc.

Such kind of Product analytics questions.
